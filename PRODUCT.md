# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One principal: a founder (Special Forces veteran) running several ventures,
using it at 0530 with coffee and again at day's end. Secondary audience:
global AWS builders reading the challenge article and repo, most of whom
never wore a uniform.

## Product Purpose

Turn task chaos into one decisive daily game plan (one mission, timed blocks,
deliberate drops, visible reserve) and get smarter every evening through a
three-question debrief. Success: the morning email and console plan are
trusted enough to actually follow, and the learned-preference profile
demonstrably changes tomorrow's plan.

## Brand Personality

Decisive, calm, quietly technical. An operations room at dawn, not a war
game. The military five-paragraph format is the backbone, never the spirit:
plain English first, every borrowed term explained, zero cosplay.

## Anti-references

- Military-themed cosplay UIs (stencil fonts, camo, HUD crosshairs, klaxons)
- Generic SaaS productivity dashboards (cream background, card grids,
  gradient CTAs, confetti on task completion)
- Gamified to-do apps (streaks, badges, points)

## Design Principles

1. The plan is the hero; everything else serves reading it fast.
2. Motion conveys state; the one ceremony is plan generation, which is real
   work and deserves to feel like it.
3. Density with calm: mono for machinery, sans for prose, serif for the one
   decision that matters.
4. Fail loudly and honestly in the UI, exactly like the backend.
5. Welcoming to all: no jargon without a plain-English companion. No emojis.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast for all content text. Full reduced-motion alternatives for
every animation. Keyboard focus visible everywhere. Screen-reader landmarks
and per-section headings. Single-user tool, but built like it has users.
